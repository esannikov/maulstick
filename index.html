The Problem

The story gets lost
in iteration.

AI production is deeply iterative — many variants per shot, many branches per scene, many revisions per Bible. Without a system that holds the whole together, the author drifts between their own decisions and the story fragments along the way.

01
Fragmented vision
Bible, script, direction, style, characters — each lives in a different doc, app, or head. Without a single source of truth, every scene renders its own slightly different version of the world.
02
Story drift across revisions
The script evolves. Direction lags two versions behind. Prompts still reference a cut scene. The final edit tells one story; the production rendered another.
03
Iteration without memory
Twenty variants per shot, five script drafts, three style tests. Which decision lives? Which was dropped, and why? Without a ledger, every revision costs context.
04
Ungrounded craft
AI renders «beautiful» by default. But which angle, which rhythm, which light was actually the right choice? Beauty without craft reasoning becomes slop by the hundredth shot.
The Product

An operating system
for AI film.

Maulstick is built on three things that don’t move — the file system you own, the human in control, and the AI agent that reads, writes, and checks. Everything else is built on top of these three.

01
File System
Every artefact — Bible, script, direction, prompts, canvas, timeline — lives as plain Markdown in an Obsidian vault. No database, no lock-in, open formats. You own every file on your own disk.
02
Human
You make every creative call. Three human gates pause the pipeline — after script, after direction, after generation. The agent never overwrites your decisions without explicit approval.
03
AI Agent
Reads files, proposes, generates, reviews. Three specialised roles collaborate inside: Producer plans and routes, Critic reviews and scores, StoryPole tracks world state.

How it works

Bible to
editor timeline.

Four stages, 17 fine-grained phases, three human approval gates. The agent never runs a stage without the upstream file it depends on — and never skips a gate.

Stage 1
Project Bible
Nine structured docs lock the universe — synopsis, vision, style, characters, world, sound, tech, rules, ontology.
Stage 2
Script & Direction
Screenplay, shot-by-shot direction, transition audit. Two human gates before generation touches a single frame.
Stage 3
Visual & Generation
Style test, prompts, canvas storyboard, shot-by-shot generation across 30+ models with character lock from the Bible.
Stage 4
Assembly & Handoff
Rough cut in-browser, subtitles, final review, one-click XML / FCPXML export to Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve.

The Quality Layer

Catch breaks
before the render.

Generation is expensive. Fixing a break after the render is more expensive. Four overlapping systems guard every project — rules, interpretation, world state, and learning.

A · Rule Review

Automated checks at three stages.

The earlier a problem is found, the cheaper it is to fix. Rule checks cover continuity, shot grammar, technical coherence.

Stage 1
Script Review
Continuity, geography, prop tracking, pacing prediction, character presence.
Stage 2
Direction Review
180° axis rule, shot size jumps, 30° rule, aesthetic coherence, prompt validation.
Stage 3
Full Review
Audiovisual quality, emotional impact, rhythm — scored across 6 FilmEval dimensions.
B · Debate Engine

Where rules end, interpretation begins.

Five independent AI reviewers analyze the script from different craft perspectives. A judge model weighs their arguments and delivers a single scored verdict.

CM
Comedy Analyst
Humor mechanics, jokes-per-minute, comedic timing, rule of three, callback patterns.
DL
Dialogue Doctor
Character voice consistency, subtext layers, natural speech, power dynamics.
ST
Structure Architect
Three-act arc, turning points, setup-payoff, act proportions, midpoint strength.
AU
Audience Proxy
Engagement map, boredom zones, hook moments, emotional peaks, recommendation score.
NR
Narration Analyst
Voice-over rhythm, narrator persona, narration-to-action ratio, grounded voice.
+ JUDGE A senior judge model weighs all five perspectives and outputs a single scored verdict with per-dimension feedback.

Market Map

Where Maulstick sits
in the landscape.

The AI video stack spans several distinct layers. Maulstick operates in a specific one — the pipeline layer between generation models and professional editing. Below are the neighbours and how the scope of each relates to ours.

Layer 1
Generation models
Runway, Kling, Flux, Sora, Veo, Seedance, Wan, Reve…
These are the engines Maulstick calls. We do not train our own models — we orchestrate existing ones, translating a shot description into the dialect each model understands.
Layer 2
Templated AI video platforms
End-to-end platforms with preset pipelines, stock avatars, canned transitions.
These ship fast workflows for short-form and social content. Our focus is narrative film with a custom Bible, style lock, and NLE handoff — so the overlap is partial and the target audience is different.
Layer 3
AI storyboarding & pre-viz
Board-only tools that turn a script into visual panels.
Specialised in one phase of pre-production. Maulstick includes storyboarding as one of seventeen phases and continues past the panel into generation, assembly, and timeline export.
Layer 4
Ad-hoc tool chain
ComfyUI, prompt docs, spreadsheets, reference folders, Frame.io, NLE plugins — glued together by hand.
The working baseline for most AI film teams today. Maulstick replaces the glue layer between these tools with a single pipeline, while still calling many of the same underlying generators.
